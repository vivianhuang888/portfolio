Case Study • May 2024

U.S. Drug Overdose Analysis

Uncovering pattern shifts in overdose deaths across demographic groups and drug types.

This project explored national overdose mortality trends to identify where risk concentrated, how patterns shifted over time, and what those shifts imply for targeted public-health response. The work combined statistical analysis with visual storytelling to translate complex trends into practical insight.

Python Regression Analysis Tableau Data Storytelling

Overview

The United States overdose crisis has evolved rapidly over the last decade, with notable shifts by age, race, gender, and drug category. This study synthesizes those trends to provide a clear narrative of who is most affected and how the epidemic has changed over time.

The primary output is an analysis framework that supports both exploration and communication: rigorous enough for statistical interpretation, and accessible enough to inform broader decision-making.

Problem / Motivation

National overdose data is high-volume and multidimensional, which makes emerging risk signals easy to miss. Static summaries often hide meaningful differences between communities.

  • Where are mortality increases concentrated demographically?
  • Which drug categories are driving the most severe trend changes?
  • Do major policy periods align with directional shifts in outcomes?

Data / Methodology

The workflow combined data preparation, statistical modeling, and visualization to move from raw records to interpretable findings.

  • Data preprocessing: standardized labels, handled missing values, and engineered analysis-ready dimensions.
  • Modeling: applied regression analysis to test demographic associations with overdose death rates.
  • Visualization: built Tableau views to compare trend trajectories across age, gender, race, and drug type.

Key Findings

  • Overdose mortality shows strong association with specific demographic segments rather than uniform national change.
  • Trend inflections around policy milestones suggest mixed effects, with improvements in some areas and continued escalation in others.
  • Later periods show stronger contribution from synthetic opioids, indicating a shift in the underlying risk landscape.

Insights / Impact

This case study reframes overdose data from isolated statistics into a cohesive story of evolving public-health risk. The analysis supports more targeted prevention strategy by highlighting where interventions may need to differ across populations and time periods.

Beyond this project, the structure can be reused as a repeatable monitoring template for future surveillance and policy evaluation work.
