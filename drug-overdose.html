U.S. Drug Overdose Analysis

Uncovering the Epidemic: Drug Overdose Deaths in the United States
Duration: May 2024
Skills: Python, Regression Analysis, Tableau, Storytelling

Description

This project investigated overdose death trends in the U.S. using demographic dimensions including age, sex, race, and drug type. We focused on identifying trend shifts and translating findings into public-health insights.

Objective / Success Metrics

  • Trend Analysis: Identify meaningful shifts by demographic segment over time.
  • Factor Association: Evaluate drivers correlated with higher death rates.
  • Policy Impact: Assess directional effects of major health-policy milestones.

Approach

  1. Data Preprocessing (Python): parsing labels, cleaning nulls, and creating analysis-ready columns.
  2. Modeling: regression-based significance checks for demographic variables.
  3. Visualization (Tableau): trend narratives for drug type, age, gender, and race.

Results

The analysis showed strong associations between overdose mortality and specific demographic groups, plus post-policy shifts that suggest mixed policy effects and substitution toward synthetic opioids in later periods.
