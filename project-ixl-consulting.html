Strategic Diagnostic and Market Expansion Roadmap for an Organic SME

— A staged, data-backed growth roadmap balancing operational feasibility with long-term scale.

Duration Spring 2025
Skills Strategic Diagnostic (Value Chain), Competitive Analysis, Product Strategy, Market Expansion Roadmapping, SME Advisory

Overview / Description

Our client, an organic food SME, faced a classic scaling challenge. Despite a strong product line and loyal customer base, high customer acquisition cost and a lack of coherent digital strategy were limiting growth.

Our team conducted a strategic diagnostic to deliver a practical, data-backed roadmap for expansion.

Objective / Success Metrics

Evaluate the current business model, identify key barriers to growth, and recommend a staged market expansion strategy that balances operational feasibility with long-term scale.

Approach

1) Strategic diagnostic

  • Applied Porter's Value Chain framework to assess how each primary activity created value and incurred cost.
  • Found that certified sourcing was a true differentiator, while digital marketing and sales were underperforming.

2) Product strategy and financial validation

  • Cross-referenced sales data with production costs.
  • Identified high-margin products for refinement and validated opportunities for new premium offerings.

3) Market segmentation and competitive benchmarking

  • Benchmarked the client against other organic competitors.
  • Found that the client's authentic organic positioning was a unique advantage not yet fully reflected in digital channels.

Results / Key Findings

  • The business had a clear brand differentiator but lacked a growth-ready marketing engine.
  • Product and cost analysis supported a more focused portfolio strategy.
  • The market expansion opportunity was strongest when phased rather than pursued all at once.

Impact / Conclusion / Recommendations

  • Phase 1: Strengthen direct-to-consumer optimization, improve local SEO, and build a stronger digital brand story.
  • Phase 2: Test regional partnerships and localized campaigns.
  • Phase 3: Expand national e-commerce presence and diversify the product line based on early market results.
