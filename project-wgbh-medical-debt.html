Medical Debt Court Filings in Massachusetts: WGBH Investigative Reporting

A court-data pipeline that identified hidden medical debt lawsuits and supported actionable newsroom reporting.

Client
Jenifer B. McKim, Investigative Editor at GBH News
Role
Lead Data Analyst
Skills
PostgreSQL, Data Engineering Pipeline, Fuzzy Matching (RapidFuzz), Regex, Client Reporting & Data Storytelling

Overview / Description

Medical debt is a major crisis, yet it remains difficult to identify in public court records because lawsuits filed by hospitals and ambulances are grouped into general consumer civil cases.

GBH News needed to uncover who was aggressively suing patients and how the medical debt landscape was shifting over time. As Lead Data Analyst, I helped architect a pipeline to sift through decades of court data and translate findings into investigative insights.

Objective / Success Metrics

Surface medical debt lawsuits from large-scale public court records, standardize fragmented healthcare entity names, and produce a reliable dataset journalists could use for reporting.

Approach

Data engineering pipeline

1) Keyword filtering

  • Used advanced PostgreSQL regex with word boundaries across 43 healthcare-related terms to reduce false positives.

2) Fuzzy string matching

  • Healthcare providers often appeared under inconsistent names.
  • Implemented normalization, stopword filtering, and RapidFuzz at an 85% similarity threshold to consolidate hundreds of provider name variations.

3) Data integration

  • Cross-referenced court data with official Massachusetts licensed ambulance registries to improve reporting accuracy.

Results / Key Findings

  • Reduced 3.7 million records into a refined dataset of around 10,500 medical debt-related cases.
  • Discovered ambulance court filings dropped 96% from 2015 to 2025, while supporting reporting suggested the debt had been outsourced to private collection agencies rather than disappearing.
  • Found litigation was concentrated among a small number of creditors, including Trinity EMS and MSPCA Angell.
  • Observed that urgent care and emergency-related cases were often resolved in a median of around 50 days, suggesting a high likelihood of default judgments.

Impact / Conclusion / Recommendations

This project showed how data engineering and data storytelling can help non-technical stakeholders uncover real-world systemic issues. It also strengthened my ability to align SQL/Python workflows with investigative reporting goals.
