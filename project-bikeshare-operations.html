Capital Bikeshare Operations Optimization

A demand and rebalancing analysis to improve bike availability while lowering operational friction.

Duration
2025
Skills
Python, Spatial-Temporal Analysis, Forecasting, Operations Planning, Data Visualization

Overview / Description

Bikeshare systems frequently face station-level shortages and surpluses that reduce rider reliability during peak windows.

This project analyzed trip patterns, weather effects, and directional commute flows to guide smarter rebalancing operations.

Objective / Success Metrics

The objective was to improve rider access with more efficient redistribution planning:

  1. Availability: Reduce stockout risk at high-demand stations.
  2. Efficiency: Minimize rebalancing mileage and avoidable overtime.
  3. Timing: Align rebalancing windows with recurring demand shifts.

Approach

1) Demand profiling

Built station-level demand signatures by hour, weekday/weekend, and seasonality effects.

  • Classified stations by source/sink behavior.
  • Mapped recurring directional commute imbalances.

2) Short-horizon planning

  • Forecasting: Estimated near-term net bike movement by cluster.
  • Routing: Proposed redistribution bundles for operations crews.

3) Performance monitoring

Defined operational KPIs for stockout minutes, empty-dock minutes, and route efficiency.

Results / Key Insights

Weekday commute corridors drove the largest directional imbalance, while weekend demand was more weather- and leisure-sensitive.

Dominant patternDirectional commute flow

Operational leverTime-bucket routing

Cluster-based rebalancing windows offered a practical balance between availability and routing effort.

Impact / Conclusion / Future Work

The framework supports repeatable shift planning and can be extended into more dynamic dispatch decisions.

  • Impact: Improved operational visibility into where and when imbalances emerge.
  • Future work: Integrate live station telemetry for intra-day optimization.
