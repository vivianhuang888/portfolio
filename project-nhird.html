health policy analytics case study

Evaluating Health Policy Impact with NHIRD

Population-level claims analysis of dementia and P4P effects on diabetes care quality.

Duration
2024–2025
Skills
Claims Data Analytics, Cohort Design, Regression Modeling, Policy Evaluation

Overview / Description

Using NHIRD records, this study measured how comorbidity and payment incentives changed care patterns for diabetic populations across longitudinal cohorts.

Objective / Success Metrics

Assess differences in utilization, continuity, and quality indicators between patients exposed and unexposed to targeted programs.

Approach

Constructed matched cohorts, applied risk adjustment, and tested sensitivity across model specifications to separate policy signal from baseline disease burden.

Results / Key Insights

P4P exposure was associated with improved follow-up consistency, while dementia burden remained a strong modifier of quality outcomes and resource needs.

Impact / Conclusion / Future Work

Findings support tailored care pathways for dual-burden patients and inform value-based program design with equity-focused risk stratification.
