health policy analytics case study

Evaluating Health Policy Impact with NHIRD

A population-level claims analysis of dementia burden and pay-for-performance effects in diabetes care.

Duration
2024-2025
Skills
Claims Analytics, Cohort Design, Regression Modeling, Policy Evaluation, R

Overview / Description

Policymakers need evidence on whether payment incentives improve outcomes for patients with complex chronic conditions.

Using NHIRD claims data, this project compared utilization and quality patterns between diabetes patients with and without dementia under different policy exposure groups.

Objective / Success Metrics

The project aimed to isolate policy effects while accounting for baseline clinical complexity:

  1. Policy signal: Estimate the association of P4P participation with follow-up quality.
  2. Equity lens: Measure whether dementia status modified program benefit.
  3. Actionability: Translate findings into targeted care management priorities.

Approach

1) Cohort construction

Built longitudinal diabetes cohorts and aligned observation windows for policy exposure and outcomes.

  • Defined comorbidity and utilization baselines before exposure.
  • Applied matching and risk adjustment to improve comparability.

2) Modeling strategy

  • Core models: Multivariable regressions for utilization and continuity metrics.
  • Sensitivity checks: Alternative specifications to test robustness of effect direction.

3) Interpretation

Converted statistical outputs into policy-facing insights for program design and high-risk subgroup management.

Results / Key Insights

P4P participation was associated with better continuity of care, but dementia burden remained a strong driver of outcome variability.

Program effectPositive

Key modifierDementia status

Findings highlighted the need for differentiated interventions rather than one-size-fits-all policy design.

Impact / Conclusion / Future Work

This analysis supports more targeted value-based strategies for dual-burden populations within national claims programs.

  • Impact: Strengthened evidence for risk-stratified policy implementation.
  • Future work: Add provider-level variation analysis and longer-term outcome tracking.
