healthcare operations case study

Tackling ED Overcrowding Through Throughput Analytics

An operations analytics project focused on reducing wait-time bottlenecks from triage through discharge.

Duration
Spring 2025
Skills
Process Mapping, Queue Analysis, SQL, KPI Design, Healthcare Operations

Overview / Description

Emergency department crowding created long waits, care delays, and high pressure on staff during peak hours.

This project analyzed patient flow from arrival to discharge to identify where delays accumulated and where improvement would create the largest throughput gains.

Objective / Success Metrics

The goal was to prioritize operational interventions with measurable effects on access and flow:

  1. Access: Reduce door-to-provider time across all acuity tiers.
  2. Flow: Shorten discharge turnaround and boarding duration.
  3. Reliability: Decrease shift-level variability in throughput.

Approach

1) Current-state diagnostics

Mapped the ED journey into triage, provider evaluation, diagnostics, disposition, and discharge handoff stages.

  • Segmented cycle times by acuity, shift, and day-of-week.
  • Identified queue build-up patterns during late-day surges.

2) Bottleneck analysis

  • Imaging dependency: Delays concentrated around diagnostic turnaround windows.
  • Discharge coordination: Last-mile handoff steps created avoidable boarding time.

3) Intervention design

Proposed fast-track eligibility rules, standardized discharge windows, and a weekly KPI review cadence for ongoing tuning.

Results / Key Insights

Analysis showed that a small number of late-stage process steps drove a large share of total length-of-stay variance.

Primary bottleneckDischarge delay

Most volatile windowLate afternoon

Targeting discharge coordination and fast-track routing emerged as the highest-yield operational strategy.

Impact / Conclusion / Future Work

The project produced a practical playbook for monthly ED throughput review and cross-team alignment on flow KPIs.

  • Impact: Turned broad crowding concerns into actionable process-level priorities.
  • Future work: Add short-horizon arrival forecasts to support proactive staffing and bed planning.
