healthcare operations case study

Tackling ED Overcrowding Through Throughput Analytics

A workflow and queue analysis project focused on reducing bottlenecks from triage through discharge.

Duration
Spring 2025
Skills
Process Mapping, Queue Analysis, KPI Design, Operations Strategy

Overview / Description

This project evaluated emergency department flow patterns to identify where wait time and boarding pressure accumulated most often.

Objective / Success Metrics

  • Reduce door-to-provider delay.
  • Lower left-without-being-seen rate.
  • Improve discharge turn-around and bed turnover consistency.

Approach

Built a current-state process map, segmented patients by acuity and disposition, and analyzed cycle time variance by shift and staffing conditions to prioritize high-leverage interventions.

Results / Key Insights

Major bottlenecks were concentrated in discharge coordination and late-day imaging turnaround. Recommendations focused on fast-track eligibility rules and standardized discharge handoff timing.

Impact / Conclusion / Future Work

The framework provides a repeatable method for monthly operations review. Future work can layer predictive arrival forecasts for proactive staffing plans.
