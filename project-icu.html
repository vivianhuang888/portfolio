healthcare analytics case study

Forecasting ICU Demand and Readmission Risk with MIMIC-IV

A unified machine learning framework connecting ED triage risk, ICU discharge safety, and short-term bed demand forecasting.

Duration
Fall 2025
Skills
Python, XGBoost, Clinical Data Analytics (MIMIC-IV), Data Visualization

Overview / Description

ED crowding and ICU strain remain major operational challenges. Patients who appear stable in the ED can deteriorate quickly, while premature ICU discharges can lead to unsafe readmissions.

This project links the full patient journey from ED triage to ICU admission, discharge, and potential return using MIMIC-IV EHR data. The goal was to shift planning from reactive bed management to near-term proactive forecasting.

Objective / Success Metrics

Rather than modeling ED-to-ICU and ICU-to-ward transitions in isolation, this work combines both into one decision-support pipeline:

  1. Early deterioration detection: Predict ICU admission within 12 hours of ED arrival.
  2. Safe discharge assessment: Predict ICU readmission within 72 hours post-discharge.
  3. Unified capacity forecasting: Combine admission and readmission risks with LOS expectations.

Approach

1) Data integration & cohort design

Merged MIMIC-IV-ED and MIMIC-IV v3.1 tables using timestamped relational joins across ed_stays, admissions, transfers, and icustays with subject_id and hadm_id.

  • Built an upstream cohort for ED-to-ICU prediction.
  • Built a downstream cohort of post-ICU survivors for readmission modeling.

2) Feature engineering

  • Medication normalization: Converted free-text home meds into structured categories (1,000+ names mapped to 22 classes).
  • Triage standardization: Parsed pain score strings and normalized top chief complaints into binary indicators.

3) Predictive modeling

Used XGBoost with class-imbalance handling via scale_pos_weight and optimized for AUC and Recall to improve high-risk sensitivity.

Results / Key Insights

Model 1: 12-hour ICU admission

AUC0.96

Recall0.93

The upstream model achieved strong discrimination and high sensitivity for early deterioration risk.

Model 2: 72-hour ICU readmission

AUC0.72

Recall0.52

Readmission remained harder to predict, but model performance supported practical early-warning use for borderline discharges.

Impact / Conclusion / Future Work

The key contribution is system-level forecasting through a proof-of-concept ICU bed demand dashboard updated in 12-hour intervals.

Current ICU beds + predicted ED inflow - expected discharges + predicted readmissions = net ICU bed demand

  • Impact: Provides an operationally practical way to anticipate bed shortages and reduce unsafe early discharge.
  • Future work: Add a dedicated LOS model and integrate unstructured notes plus physiologic time-series to improve readmission precision.
