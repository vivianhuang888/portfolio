Bridging the Gap: Forecasting ICU Demand and Readmission Risk Using MIMIC Data

A unified machine learning framework that links ED triage risk, ICU discharge safety, and short-term capacity forecasting into one operational view.

Duration
Fall 2025
Skills
Data Preprocessing (Python), Machine Learning (XGBoost), Data Visualization, Clinical Data Analytics (MIMIC-IV)

Overview / Description

Emergency department (ED) crowding and intensive care unit (ICU) strain remain major operational challenges. Patients who appear stable in the ED can deteriorate quickly, while premature ICU discharges can lead to dangerous readmissions.

This project addresses short-term ICU capacity planning using MIMIC-IV EHR data. By tracking the full patient journey from ED triage to ICU admission, discharge, and potential return, we built two complementary predictive models that feed a unified ICU bed demand forecasting framework.

Objective / Success Metrics

Rather than treating ED-to-ICU transfers and ICU-to-ward discharges as separate tasks, this project integrates both transitions into one decision-support pipeline.

  1. Early Deterioration Detection (Phase 1): Predict ICU admission within 12 hours of ED arrival using early triage features.
  2. Safe Discharge Assessment (Phase 2): Predict ICU readmission within 72 hours using pre-discharge clinical data.
  3. Unified Capacity Forecasting: Combine admission and readmission predictions with Length of Stay (LOS) estimates to project near-term ICU demand.

Approach

1) Data Integration & Cohort Selection (Python)

We integrated MIMIC-IV-ED and MIMIC-IV v3.1 using timestamped relational joins across ed_stays, admissions, transfers, and icustays with subject_id and hadm_id.

  • Built an upstream cohort for ED-to-ICU prediction.
  • Built a downstream cohort of post-ICU survivors for readmission modeling.

2) Feature Engineering & Unstructured Data Handling

  • Medication text normalization: Converted free-text home medication records into structured signals, mapping 1,000+ unique drug names to 22 pharmacologic categories.
  • Triage data standardization: Parsed free-text pain scores and normalized top 25 chief complaints into binary indicators.

3) Predictive Modeling (XGBoost)

Because ICU admission and readmission are imbalanced events, we used XGBoost with scale_pos_weight adjustments and prioritized AUC and Recall to improve sensitivity for high-risk patients.

Results / Key Insights

Model 1: 12-Hour ICU Admission (Upstream)

AUC: 0.96 Recall: 0.93

The upstream model achieved strong discrimination and high sensitivity, identifying most patients likely to require urgent intensive care based on early ED signals.

Model 2: 72-Hour ICU Readmission (Downstream)

AUC: 0.72 Recall: 0.52

Readmission prediction is inherently harder due to post-ICU recovery factors, but performance was sufficient to support a meaningful early-warning screen for borderline discharges.

Impact / Conclusion / Future Work

Beyond individual predictions, the key contribution is system-level forecasting through a proof-of-concept ICU Bed Demand Dashboard running on 12-hour intervals.

Current ICU Beds + Predicted ED Inflow - Expected Discharges + Predicted Readmissions = Net ICU Bed Demand

  • Impact: Provides an operationally practical way to anticipate bed shortages, prioritize triage decisions, and reduce unsafe early discharge.
  • Future work: Add a dedicated LOS model and integrate unstructured clinical notes plus physiologic time-series data to improve readmission precision.
