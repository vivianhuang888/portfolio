Bridging the Gap: Forecasting ICU Demand and Readmission Risk

Built a machine learning framework to predict short-term ICU demand by combining ED-to-ICU admission risk and ICU readmission risk.

Machine Learning Healthcare Analytics ICU Forecasting XGBoost EHR Data Operations Analytics

Overview

Hospitals often struggle to anticipate ICU demand in real time. Patients may deteriorate quickly in the ED, while others return shortly after ICU discharge — both creating pressure on limited ICU capacity.

This project connects these two challenges and frames them as a short-term ICU forecasting problem, rather than separate prediction tasks.

Objectives

  • Predict ICU admission within 12 hours from ED data
  • Predict ICU readmission within 72 hours after discharge
  • Combine both into a short-term ICU demand forecasting framework

Data & Method

  • Data: MIMIC-IV (ED + ICU + hospital data)
  • Integration: Linked patient journey using hadm_id across ED → ICU → discharge
  • Feature engineering:
    • Triage signals (vitals, acuity, complaints)
    • Medication grouping (1,000+ → 22 categories)
  • Model: XGBoost with imbalance handling

Results

ICU Admission Model

  • AUC: 0.96
  • Recall: 0.93

ICU Readmission Model

  • AUC: 0.72
  • Recall: 0.52

Key insight:

Early ED data is highly predictive, while readmission is inherently more complex but still useful as a screening signal.

ICU Forecasting Framework

  • Inflow: predicted ICU admissions from ED
  • Occupancy: estimated using ICU length of stay
  • Risk: predicted readmission after discharge

Together, this provides a real-time view of ICU demand and helps anticipate bed needs in the next 12 hours.

Impact

  • Supports ICU bed planning and resource allocation
  • Enables earlier intervention for high-risk patients
  • Adds decision support for safer ICU discharge

Key value:

Transforms machine learning predictions into operational insights, not just model outputs.
