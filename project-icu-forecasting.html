Forecasting Real-time ICU Demand

Machine Learning EHR Data (MIMIC-IV) Operations

Bridging the gap in capacity planning by forecasting short-term ICU patient flow and readmission risk to alleviate hospital bed strain.

The Challenge

Anticipating Capacity Bottlenecks

Hospitals often face sudden surges in Intensive Care Unit (ICU) bed demand. Patients who initially appear stable in the ED may deteriorate, while premature ICU discharges can lead to rapid readmissions.

The challenge was to move away from reactive bed management and develop a data-driven approach to anticipate short-term capacity needs, ensuring patient safety and optimal resource allocation.

The Approach & Solution

A Unified Forecasting Framework

Utilizing the MIMIC-IV electronic health record database, we developed two complementary machine learning models using XGBoost. The first model flags high-risk ED patients likely to require ICU transfer within 12 hours. The second assesses 72-hour readmission risk for those being discharged.

Business Impact: We integrated these predictive models into a dynamic ICU capacity dashboard, providing hospital administrators with real-time visibility into expected inflows and outflows, ultimately enabling proactive staffing and safer discharge planning.
